Situation


Does temperature decrease on earth as one moves further away from the equator?
Although the question may invoke a 'duh' response, how can it be proven?
Also, how are other weather measurements affected by the distance from the equator?


Objective


The objective was to prove that in general temperatures decrease on earth as one moves further from the equator.
A secondary objective is to assess the potential relationship between distance from the equator and other weather measurements.


Method


To test our hypothesis we:
  1. Selected a random point on the earth
  2. Generated a set of uniformly distributed pairs of latitudes and longitudes
  3. Selected the nearest city in the area around each point, if any
  4. Obtained current wether data for each of over 500 cities for comparison
    • Temperature
    • Humidity
    • Wind Speed
    • Cloud Cover
  5. Plotted the weather measurement vs. latitude on a graph.
  6. Conducted statistical analyses of the results, where correlation was above 5%.
