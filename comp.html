Temperature Analysis



Statistical Method #1


We will test to see if there is a signficant difference in the temperatures within and beyond 45 degrees latitude from the equator.

Hypothesis: The temperature more than 45 degrees latitude from the equator is less than the temperature within 45 degrees latitude of the equator. μ45+<μ45-
Null Hypothesis: Temperature more than 45 degrees latitude from the equator is greater than or equal to the temperature within 45 degrees latitude of the equator. μ45+≥μ45-
Significance Level: 1%.
Test Method: One-tailed, two-sample t-test
Statistical Analysis:

Statistical Method #2


We will test the fit of temperature as a function of cosine of latitude to temperture being a constant.

Hypothesis: The temperature is a function of latitude approximated by -44.7+125.3×cos(lat).
Alternative Hypothesis: The temperature is independent of the cosine of the latitude.
Significance level: 1%
Test Method: Fisher’s F-test
Statistical Analysis: Let y = -44.7+125.3×cos(lat)
SSEx (sum of squares of errors for constant assumption) =896866.5
SSEy (sum of squares of errors for ys) = 90346.9
DF = 563 (use 120 in the F-table, for a conservative answer) There are 2 parameters selected, the intercept and multiplier (slope); so, DF=n-2.

Test Statistic: F=(SSEx – SSEy)/( SSEy/563) = 5025.9

Interpretation: To pass the F-test at a 1% significance, F must be greater than 1.533,
which it does. Therefore, we are at least 99% certain the model -44.7 + 125.3 × cos(latitude) for temperature
is more accurate than assuming the temperature is 53.8 regardless of latitude.

[Note: By further F-tests, a linear regression to the absolute value of the latitude also passes the F-test at 99% certainty,
but the cosine model is at least 99% likely to be a better model than the linear regression model.]
