Comparison of Weather to Latitude



Statistical Results


METHOD A

Hypothesis: The temperature more than 45 degrees latitude from the equator is less than the temperature within 45 degrees latitude of the equator. μ 45+ < μ 45- Null Hypothesis: Temperature more than 45 degrees latitude from the equator is the less than or equal to the temperature within 45 degrees latitude of the equator. μ 45+ ≥ μ 45- Significance Level: 1%. Test Method: Two-sample t-test Statistical Analysis: S45+ = 20.10 n45+ = 159 ẋ45+ = 17.565 S45- = 15.44 n45- = 406 ẋ45- = 67.953 SE (standard error) = √{[( S245+ (n45+-1) + S245- (n45--1))/( n45+ + n45- -2)][1/ n45+ + 1/ n45+]}= 1.579 DF (degrees of freedom) = ( S245+/ n45+ + S245-/ n45-)2/{[( S245+/ n45+ )2/(n45+-1)]+ [( S245-/ n45- )2/(n45--1)]} = 235 Test Statistic: t = (ẋ45+ - ẋ45-)/SE = -31.91 >P-value: The p-value is less than 0.00001. Interpretation: Reject the null hypothesis. Based on the data provided there is a greater than 99.999% chance that the temperature more than 45 degrees latitude from the equator is less than the temperature within 45 degrees of the equator.

METHOD B

Hypothesis: The temperature is a function of latitude approximated by -44.7+125.3×cos(lat). Alternative Hypothesis: The temperature is independent of the cosine of the latitude. Significance level: 1% Test Method: Fisher’s F-test Statistical Analysis: Let y = -44.7+125.3×cos(lat) SSEx (sum of squares of errors for constant assumption) =896866.5 SSEy (sum of squares of errors for ys) = 90346.93 DF = 564 (use 120 in the F-table, for conservatism) Test Statistic: F=(SSEx – SSEy)/( SSEy/564) = 5034.7 Interpretation: To pass the F-test at a 1% significance, F must be greater than 1.533, which it does. Therefore, we are at least 99% certain the model -44.7 + 125.3 x cos(latitude) for temperature is more accurate than assuming the temperature is 53.8 regardless of latitude. [Note: By further F-tests, a linear regression to the absolute value of the latitude also passes the F-test at 99% certainty, but the cosine model is at least 99% likely to be a better model than the linear regression model.]

CONCLUSION

The temperature on the earth decreases as the distance from the equator increases. The average temperature (°F) at a latitude can be approximated by -44.7 + 125.3 × cos(latitude).
